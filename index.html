The problem

Inserting and removing hard contact lenses
shouldn't be difficult.

7.5M+ people in the US wear hard contact lenses
3 in 10 patients give up on hard lenses
80% report high difficulty inserting or removing lenses, due to fear
11% scratched their eyes during insertion or removal

First-time users

Getting started is the hardest part. The eye fights back, blinking and watering the moment a finger comes close. Lenses get dropped, flipped, or scratched against the cornea. It often takes hour-long appointments just to learn, and many people give up before it ever feels natural.

Limited mobility

Steady hands are not a given. Arthritis, tremors, and weak grip make it nearly impossible to pinch a tiny lens and hold the eyelid open at the same time. For many, that means leaning on a caregiver every day, or giving up on hard lenses altogether.
